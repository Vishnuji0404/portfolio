Projects

  • Project 1: REAL WORLD ENTREPRENEURSHIP
  • We began by brainstorming potential business ideas that would cater to our target customers—individuals living in hostels or as paying guests. After considering several options, including setting up food stalls and selling handcrafted goods, we decided to focus on selling essential everyday items such as baskets, containers, water bottles, hair clips, scrunchies, socks, and handkerchiefs. This choice was made based on the need for cost-effective, high-demand products that would be easy to source and quick to sell. The project was executed in phases, starting with a market visit to Hatibagan Market in Kolkata, where we purchased our initial stock after negotiating with vendors. Our sales strategy included both online and offline promotions, targeting hostel and PG residents through word-of-mouth marketing, WhatsApp groups, and personal networks. We managed our finances carefully, tracking every transaction to ensure profitability while keeping prices competitive to attract more customers. Despite several challenges—such as coordinating group efforts, managing finances, and balancing academic schedules—our team implemented strategic measures to overcome these hurdles. We successfully completed three phases of product purchases and sales, gradually increasing our revenue and reinvesting profits into subsequent inventory rounds. By the end of the project, we turned our initial investment of ₹1000 into a net profit of ₹1214.50. This project provided us with invaluable insights into the fundamentals of entrepreneurship, including the importance of teamwork, market research, strategic planning, and financial discipline. Overall, it was a highly educational and rewarding experience that has prepared us to tackle similar business challenges in the future.

  • Explanation:
    • This research thrust area focuses on how entrepreneurship can thrive with minimal capital investment, especially in developing economies. The "1000 Rupees Challenge" offers a real-world scenario where students or entrepreneurs are tasked with starting a business using just a small amount of capital.

    >Creative and frugal business strategies. The role of microfinance and crowdfunding in empowering small-scale entrepreneurs. How digital platforms can lower startup costs. The psychology behind entrepreneurial decision-making in resource-limited environments. This type of research is crucial for developing sustainable business models that work in emerging markets or for underprivileged populations, encouraging innovation under financial constraints.

  • Explanation:

The "Poster Making Competition for Invitation" highlights the importance of design elements in marketing and promotional activities.

    >The psychology of colors, shapes, and typography in influencing human perception and behavior.
